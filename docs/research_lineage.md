# Research Lineage

This repository is part of a single research line connecting near-critical theory, controlled benchmarking, and industrial Digital Twin application.

## 1. Near-Critical Systems / Paper A

The first research layer is the foundational Near-Critical Systems framework.

It introduced the core conceptual machinery:

- near-critical operating regimes
- stability margin / distance-to-collapse logic
- threshold-control interpretation
- first-passage / collapse-risk reasoning
- diffusion approximation and stochastic modeling
- Monte Carlo validation

Repository:

- https://github.com/net421/near-critical-systems

## 2. Controlled Near-Critical Benchmark / Paper B

This repository contains the Paper B benchmark layer.

It uses a controlled simulation environment to test how preventive threshold-control logic behaves under reproducible conditions before or while being transferred to an industrial Digital Twin setting.

The benchmark focuses on:

- policy comparison
- oracle-threshold discovery
- cost-ratio boundaries
- physical/economic criticality
- reproducible evidence for the Paper B manuscript

## 3. Supply Chain Digital Twin Application

The industrial Digital Twin project applies and extends the near-critical framework to a networked supply-chain setting.

It introduces:

- multi-echelon supply-chain simulation
- network-aware policies
- resilience analytics
- decision-support dashboards
- industrial-scale experimentation

Repository:

- https://github.com/net421/Supply-Chain-Digital-Twin

## Correct interpretation

Paper B should not be treated as a separate unrelated project.

The correct interpretation is:

```text
Near-Critical Systems theory
        ↓
Controlled benchmark evidence
        ↓
Industrial Digital Twin application
```

The benchmark and Digital Twin work were developed as connected applications of the same near-critical framework. Paper B captures the results and reproducibility package for that controlled near-critical benchmark and its role in supporting the Digital Twin use case.
